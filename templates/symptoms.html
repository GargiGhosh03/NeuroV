Brain Tumor Symptoms

Recognizing the warning signs early can save lives

Common Symptoms

Brain tumors can cause a variety of symptoms depending on their type, location, and growth rate. These symptoms may develop gradually or appear suddenly. Below are some commonly reported signs:

  • Persistent or worsening headaches, often in the morning
  • Unexplained nausea or vomiting
  • Vision problems (blurred or double vision, loss of peripheral vision)
  • Seizures, especially without a prior history
  • Difficulty with balance or walking
  • Speech difficulties or confusion
  • Personality or behavioral changes
  • Memory problems or cognitive decline
  • Weakness or numbness in limbs, often on one side of the body
  • Hearing loss or ringing in the ears
