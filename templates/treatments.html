Brain Tumor Treatments

Understanding available options and making informed decisions

Overview

Treatment for brain tumors depends on several factors including the type, size, location of the tumor, and the patient's overall health. A combination of therapies is often used for better outcomes. This page outlines the most common treatment options.

Common Treatment Methods

  • Surgery: Surgical removal of the tumor is often the first step when accessible and safe. It helps reduce tumor size and relieve pressure.
  • Radiation Therapy: Uses high-energy rays to destroy cancer cells or shrink tumors. Common after surgery or when surgery isn’t an option.
  • Chemotherapy: Drug therapy used to kill tumor cells, typically delivered orally or intravenously.
  • Targeted Therapy: Drugs that target specific genes or proteins that contribute to cancer growth.
  • Immunotherapy: Boosts the body's natural defenses to fight cancer using substances made by the body or in a lab.

Supportive & Palliative Care

Treatments may also include palliative care to improve quality of life — managing symptoms like seizures, pain, fatigue, and emotional distress. Rehabilitation services such as physical, occupational, and speech therapy may be part of the recovery process.
